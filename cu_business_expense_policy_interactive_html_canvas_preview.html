AY2026 Spending Guidelines
Quick Reference Guide

⚠ Fiscal Stewardship GuidanceOTPS · Faculty Funds · Travel · Hiring · Major Purchases
Columbia Engineering’s AY2026 spending posture is rooted in careful stewardship. Departments and faculty are asked to align spending with academic and research priorities, preserve resources where possible, and prioritize expenses that are essential to advancing the School’s mission.
Executive Standard
Core Operating Principle
Proceed When
  • The expense is essential to research, education, student support, or core operations.
  • Funding is available, authorized, and aligned with the intended purpose.
  • The request can be justified clearly and documented before commitment.
  • The purchase or action cannot reasonably be delayed.
Pause When
  • The expense is discretionary, social, cosmetic, or convenience-based.
  • The budget impact is unclear or funding is uncertain.
  • The request is over a stated threshold and has not been reviewed.
  • The activity can be delayed until the funding landscape is more stable.
Leadership Message

Use the same discipline you would use in a closeout review: confirm purpose, confirm funding, confirm timing, then proceed only when the mission case is clear.

Operational Spending
OTPS Controls
Faculty and departments should continue exercising prudence in operational and non-personnel spending. Departments are requested to reduce budgets by at least 10% to preserve resources and support transition funding amid University-wide uncertainty.
Operational Review Lens
Mission NecessityIs the expense essential to research, teaching, student support, compliance, or core operations?
TimingCan this cost be deferred without damaging academic or research outcomes?
Funding RiskDoes the funding source support this use, and is there enough budget capacity?
Budget VisibilityHas the Department DFA and Dean’s Office been informed when required?
Faculty Funds
Start-up & Discretionary Funds
Allowed Focus
  • Expenses essential to advancing research and education.
  • Time-sensitive academic or research needs.
  • Approved major expenses with documented rationale.
  • Spending aligned with the purpose of the faculty account.
Requires Review
  • Major expenses of $20K or more.
  • General purchases over $5K.
  • Scholarly allowance or start-up commitments with material budget impact.
  • Items that are discretionary, deferrable, or not mission-critical.
Flexibility Granted
Faculty start-up funds scheduled to expire in FY25 or FY26 will receive a one-year extension to provide additional flexibility during this period of constrained spending.
Events & Entertainment
Social Events and Gatherings

Departments are asked to be conservative with entertainment and social events. Events should be limited in scope and cost, and prioritized when they directly support students, faculty, or staff.

Department or faculty retreatsOn campusHold on campus to reduce spending.
Holiday partiesConservative reviewLimit scope and ensure alignment with fiscal guidance.
Decision Rule

A social event should have a clear University purpose, a controlled cost, and a direct connection to students, faculty, staff, recruitment, donor engagement, or mission support.

Major Purchases
Pre-Approval Standard
Any planned major expense of $20,000 or above requires approval. The request should explain why the item is critical and cannot be delayed.
Covered Categories
EquipmentResearch, lab, instructional, or operational equipment.
SuppliesHigh-value or bulk supply purchases with material budget impact.
ContractsVendor commitments, service agreements, consulting, or other contractual obligations.
Furniture / Office RefreshesFurniture, office refreshers, and space-related improvements.
Expected Review Window
Requests sent to seasfinance@columbia.edu will be reviewed with a response expected within 5 business days.
Travel Controls
Travel Guidelines
May Continue
  • Sponsored research travel that is critical to the research.
  • Travel approved by the sponsor.
  • Travel budgeted with sufficient funds within the project.
  • Exceptional travel requests approved by the SEAS Vice Dean of Research.
Delay / Halt
  • Unrestricted-funded travel when delay or cancellation is possible.
  • Staff development travel until conditions are more stable.
  • Travel outside guidelines without documented review and approval.

Practical Checklist
Before You Commit Funds
Approval Readiness
PurposeCan the requester explain how this supports research, education, students, faculty, staff, or essential operations?
FundingIs the funding source available, authorized, and appropriate for the expense?
ThresholdDoes the expense exceed $5K, $20K, or a per-person event threshold?
TimingCan the request be delayed without operational or mission harm?
DocumentationIs the business rationale strong enough for Dean’s Office, HR, sponsor, or audit review?
The Core Standard: Spend only when the mission case is clear, the funding is available, the timing is justified, and the approval path has been followed. When uncertain, route the request through the Department DFA and seasfinance@columbia.edu before committing funds.
